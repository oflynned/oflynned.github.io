Raw Materials

Land use changes. Deforestation and mining, for the purposes extracting raw materials, increase the amount of CO2 in our atmosphere by removing existing carbon sinks! These, indirect emissions, caused when gathering raw materials, are calculated in PlusPlant’s carbon emissions assessment.

Energy Usage

The primary culprit in terms of global CO2 emissions, is energy usage. Different energy sources (coal, oil, gas etc.) have different carbon footprints and are often used for different things (the use of machinery, heating, lighting etc.). PlantPlant comprehensively assesses and monitors the unique energy usage involved in our clients’ activities.

CO2 Emissions from Transport

Aside from the carbon emissions of day to day activities, considerable quantities of CO2 are also emitted when transporting goods. PlusPlant also assesses the CO2 emitted in transporting materials, prior to manufacture or packaging and in the post manufacture distribution of products.

A Continuous Process…

When we are sure that we have truly accounted for the carbon emissions of our clients’. We provide clients with a comprehensive report detailing their unique CO2 emissions and make recommendations as to how they can best reduce their environmental impacts. We then continue to work closely we our clients as we start to plant forestry and offset emissions.

Carbon Sink or Source?

It is important to note that while trees and soils absorb CO2, they also release some CO2 back into the atmosphere. At PlusPlant we actively manage and monitor our forests to ensure that our trees grow in optimal conditions for net CO2 sequestration. This means that trees are naturally well fertilized, appropriately drained or irrigated and experience minimal soil disturbance.

Our Guarantee

An amount of error naturally remains in our predictions and observations. At PlusPlant we are committed to being truly Carbon Neutral if not carbon negative – that is why we plant more trees than we need to, to cover all uncertainties!
